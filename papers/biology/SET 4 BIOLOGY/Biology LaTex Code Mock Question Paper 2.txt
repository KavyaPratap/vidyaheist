\section*{Question Paper}

\subsection*{Section A: Multi-Concept (5 Questions)}
\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]
\item A recombinant DNA experiment inserts a human insulin gene into \textit{Saccharomyces cerevisiae}. Which observation confirms successful expression?  
(A) Yeast grows faster on glucose medium  
(B) Yeast secretes functional insulin detected by ELISA  
(C) Yeast colonies turn blue on X-gal plates  
(D) Yeast shows antibiotic resistance

\item A mutation in the sodium-potassium pump gene affects neuron function. Which physiological effect is most likely?  
(A) Failure of resting membrane potential maintenance  
(B) Excessive neurotransmitter release  
(C) Increased muscle contraction strength  
(D) Enhanced synaptic plasticity

\item In a dihybrid cross AaBb × AaBb, if genes are linked with 30\% recombination frequency, what fraction of progeny will be recombinant?  
(A) 15\%  
(B) 30\%  
(C) 50\%  
(D) 70\%

\item A patient with thyroid dysfunction shows low TSH but high thyroxine levels. Which endocrine feedback mechanism explains this?  
(A) Positive feedback on pituitary  
(B) Negative feedback on pituitary  
(C) Lack of feedback regulation  
(D) Hypersecretion of ACTH

\item In a population of \textit{Drosophila}, allele frequency of A = 0.7 and a = 0.3. Under Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium, what is expected frequency of heterozygotes?  
(A) 0.21  
(B) 0.42  
(C) 0.49  
(D) 0.09
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Section B: Data/Experimental (5 Questions)}
\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]
\setcounter{enumi}{5}
\item A DNA replication experiment with heavy nitrogen (N$^{15}$) shows intermediate density bands after one generation. Which model does this support?  
(A) Conservative (B) Semi-conservative (C) Dispersive (D) Random

\item An experiment measures action potential in squid giant axon. When extracellular K$^+$ concentration is increased, the graph shows:  
(A) Reduced resting potential magnitude  
(B) Increased depolarization amplitude  
(C) No change in repolarization  
(D) Enhanced hyperpolarization

\item A population of yeast shows exponential growth until glucose is depleted, then growth slows. Which phase is reached?  
(A) Lag phase  
(B) Log phase  
(C) Stationary phase  
(D) Decline phase

\item A restriction digestion of plasmid yields fragments of 5 kb and 3 kb. Which technique confirms fragment sizes?  
(A) SDS-PAGE  
(B) Agarose gel electrophoresis  
(C) ELISA  
(D) Western blot

\item Photosynthetic rate in isolated chloroplasts is measured under blue light vs. green light. Which observation is correct?  
(A) Blue light supports higher rate than green  
(B) Green light supports higher rate than blue  
(C) Both equal  
(D) Neither supports photosynthesis
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Section C: Assertion-Reason / Matching (5 Questions)}
\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]
\setcounter{enumi}{10}
\item Assertion (A): DNA ligase joins Okazaki fragments.  
Reason (R): It seals nicks in sugar-phosphate backbone.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true

\item Assertion (A): ADH secretion decreases during overhydration.  
Reason (R): ADH promotes water reabsorption in collecting ducts.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true

\item Match the following:  
1. Gel electrophoresis $\rightarrow$ (a) DNA fragment separation  
2. ELISA $\rightarrow$ (b) Antigen-antibody detection  
3. Western blot $\rightarrow$ (c) Protein identification  
4. DNA microarray $\rightarrow$ (d) Gene expression profiling  

Correct match:  
(A) 1-a, 2-b, 3-c, 4-d  
(B) 1-b, 2-a, 3-d, 4-c  
(C) 1-c, 2-d, 3-a, 4-b  
(D) 1-a, 2-c, 3-b, 4-d

\item Assertion (A): Density-independent factors regulate population growth.  
Reason (R): Earthquakes affect populations regardless of density.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true

\item Assertion (A): In lac operon, glucose presence suppresses lactose utilization.  
Reason (R): Catabolite repression reduces cAMP levels, preventing CAP binding.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true
\end{enumerate}
