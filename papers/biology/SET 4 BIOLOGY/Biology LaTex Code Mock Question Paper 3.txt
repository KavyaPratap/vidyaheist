\section*{Question Paper}

\subsection*{Section A: Multi-Concept (5 Questions)}
\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]
\item A genetically engineered \textit{Arabidopsis} plant expresses a bacterial nitrogenase gene. Which physiological change would confirm functional integration?  
(A) Increased nitrate uptake from soil  
(B) Ability to fix atmospheric N$_2$ in root nodules  
(C) Enhanced photosynthetic rate under red light  
(D) Increased transpiration rate

\item A mutation in the CFTR gene affects chloride ion transport. Which combined physiological effect is observed?  
(A) Thick mucus secretion + impaired gas exchange  
(B) Excessive sweating + hyperthyroidism  
(C) Increased bone density + reduced calcium absorption  
(D) Enhanced immune response + anemia

\item In a trihybrid cross AaBbCc × AaBbCc, what fraction of progeny will be homozygous recessive for all three traits?  
(A) 1/64  
(B) 1/32  
(C) 1/16  
(D) 1/8

\item A patient with adrenal cortex dysfunction shows low cortisol but high ACTH. Which feedback mechanism explains this?  
(A) Negative feedback failure due to adrenal insufficiency  
(B) Positive feedback on pituitary  
(C) Hypersecretion of thyroxine  
(D) Lack of hypothalamic regulation

\item In a population of \textit{Drosophila}, allele frequency of A = 0.6 and a = 0.4. Under Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium, what is expected frequency of heterozygotes?  
(A) 0.24  
(B) 0.36  
(C) 0.48  
(D) 0.16
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Section B: Data/Experimental (5 Questions)}
\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]
\setcounter{enumi}{5}
\item A Meselson–Stahl experiment shows DNA bands at intermediate density after one generation. Which replication model does this support?  
(A) Conservative (B) Semi-conservative (C) Dispersive (D) Random

\item An experiment records action potentials in mammalian neurons. When extracellular Na$^+$ concentration is reduced, the graph shows:  
(A) Lower depolarization amplitude  
(B) Increased repolarization rate  
(C) No change in hyperpolarization  
(D) Enhanced resting potential

\item A population of yeast shows logistic growth. At which phase is carrying capacity reached?  
(A) Lag phase  
(B) Exponential phase  
(C) Stationary phase  
(D) Decline phase

\item A restriction digestion of plasmid yields fragments of 6 kb and 2 kb. Which technique confirms fragment sizes?  
(A) SDS-PAGE  
(B) Agarose gel electrophoresis  
(C) ELISA  
(D) Western blot

\item Photosynthetic rate in isolated chloroplasts is measured under far-red light vs. white light. Which observation is correct?  
(A) Far-red excites only PSI, reducing rate  
(B) Far-red excites only PSII, increasing rate  
(C) White light excites both PSI and PSII  
(D) Far-red inhibits Calvin cycle
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Section C: Assertion-Reason / Matching (5 Questions)}
\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]
\setcounter{enumi}{10}
\item Assertion (A): DNA polymerase III has proofreading activity.  
Reason (R): It removes mismatched nucleotides via 3'?5' exonuclease.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true

\item Assertion (A): Aldosterone secretion increases during low blood sodium.  
Reason (R): Aldosterone promotes Na$^+$ reabsorption in distal tubule.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true

\item Match the following:  
1. RT-PCR $\rightarrow$ (a) RNA amplification via cDNA  
2. ELISA $\rightarrow$ (b) Antigen-antibody detection  
3. Western blot $\rightarrow$ (c) Protein identification  
4. FISH $\rightarrow$ (d) Chromosomal DNA localization  

Correct match:  
(A) 1-a, 2-b, 3-c, 4-d  
(B) 1-b, 2-a, 3-d, 4-c  
(C) 1-c, 2-d, 3-a, 4-b  
(D) 1-a, 2-c, 3-b, 4-d

\item Assertion (A): Density-dependent factors regulate population growth.  
Reason (R): Competition for resources intensifies with density.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true

\item Assertion (A): In lac operon, glucose presence suppresses lactose utilization.  
Reason (R): Catabolite repression reduces cAMP, preventing CAP binding.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true
\end{enumerate}
