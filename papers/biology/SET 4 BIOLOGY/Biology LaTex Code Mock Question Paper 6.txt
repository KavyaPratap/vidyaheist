\section*{Question Paper}

\subsection*{Section A: Multi-Concept (5 Questions)}
\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]
\item A transgenic \textit{Solanum tuberosum} plant expresses a gene for drought tolerance. Which physiological change confirms success?  
(A) Increased stomatal opening under drought  
(B) Enhanced proline accumulation and osmotic adjustment  
(C) Higher transpiration rate  
(D) Reduced photosynthetic efficiency

\item A mutation in acetylcholine receptor gene affects neuromuscular junction. Which combined physiological effect is observed?  
(A) Impaired muscle contraction + reduced synaptic transmission  
(B) Enhanced neurotransmitter release + hyperthyroidism  
(C) Increased bone density + reduced calcium absorption  
(D) Excessive sweating + anemia

\item In a dihybrid cross AaBb × AaBb, if recombination frequency is 20\%, what fraction of progeny will be recombinant?  
(A) 10\%  
(B) 20\%  
(C) 40\%  
(D) 50\%

\item A patient with adrenal medulla tumor shows excess adrenaline secretion. Which physiological effect is most likely?  
(A) Hypoglycemia and reduced heart rate  
(B) Hyperglycemia and increased heart rate  
(C) Hypothyroidism and low blood pressure  
(D) Hypercortisolism and obesity

\item In a population of \textit{Drosophila}, allele frequency of A = 0.4 and a = 0.6. Under Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium, what is expected frequency of heterozygotes?  
(A) 0.24  
(B) 0.36  
(C) 0.48  
(D) 0.60
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Section B: Data/Experimental (5 Questions)}
\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]
\setcounter{enumi}{5}
\item A Meselson–Stahl experiment shows DNA bands at intermediate density after one generation. Which replication model does this support?  
(A) Conservative (B) Semi-conservative (C) Dispersive (D) Random

\item An experiment records action potentials in mammalian neurons. When extracellular Na$^+$ concentration is reduced, the graph shows:  
(A) Lower depolarization amplitude  
(B) Increased repolarization rate  
(C) No change in hyperpolarization  
(D) Enhanced resting potential

\item A bacterial population shows logistic growth. At which phase is carrying capacity reached?  
(A) Lag phase  
(B) Exponential phase  
(C) Stationary phase  
(D) Decline phase

\item A restriction digestion of plasmid yields fragments of 8 kb and 2 kb. Which technique confirms fragment sizes?  
(A) SDS-PAGE  
(B) Agarose gel electrophoresis  
(C) ELISA  
(D) Western blot

\item Photosynthetic rate in isolated chloroplasts is measured under red light vs. blue light. Which observation is correct?  
(A) Blue light supports higher rate than red  
(B) Red light supports higher rate than blue  
(C) Both equal  
(D) Neither supports photosynthesis
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Section C: Assertion-Reason / Matching (5 Questions)}
\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]
\setcounter{enumi}{10}
\item Assertion (A): DNA topoisomerase prevents supercoiling during replication.  
Reason (R): It introduces transient breaks in DNA strands.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true

\item Assertion (A): ADH secretion increases during dehydration.  
Reason (R): ADH promotes water reabsorption in collecting ducts.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true

\item Match the following:  
1. RT-PCR $\rightarrow$ (a) RNA amplification via cDNA  
2. ELISA $\rightarrow$ (b) Antigen-antibody detection  
3. Western blot $\rightarrow$ (c) Protein identification  
4. DNA sequencing $\rightarrow$ (d) Determines nucleotide order  

Correct match:  
(A) 1-a, 2-b, 3-c, 4-d  
(B) 1-b, 2-a, 3-d, 4-c  
(C) 1-c, 2-d, 3-a, 4-b  
(D) 1-a, 2-c, 3-b, 4-d

\item Assertion (A): Density-independent factors regulate population growth.  
Reason (R): Earthquakes affect populations regardless of density.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true

\item Assertion (A): In lac operon, lactose acts as inducer.  
Reason (R): Lactose binds repressor, preventing operator binding.  
(A) Both A and R true, R correct explanation  
(B) Both A and R true, R not explanation  
(C) A true, R false  
(D) A false, R true
\end{enumerate}
