\section*{Biology Set 3 - Paper 3 (Questions)}

\begin{enumerate}
\item [Diversity] Which feature explains why *Urochordata* are considered chordates despite adult forms lacking a notochord?  
(A) Presence of notochord in larval stage  
(B) Presence of radial symmetry in adults  
(C) Segmentation of body muscles  
(D) Presence of jointed appendages

\item [Cell Biology] In eukaryotic cells, which complex of the electron transport chain pumps protons into the intermembrane space but does not directly reduce oxygen?  
(A) Complex I  
(B) Complex II  
(C) Complex III  
(D) Complex IV

\item [Genetics] In a dihybrid cross AaBb × AaBb, if genes are linked with 10% recombination frequency, what proportion of offspring will show recombinant phenotypes?  
(A) 10%  
(B) 20%  
(C) 40%  
(D) 50%

\item [Plant Physiology] Which mechanism explains why C\(_4\) plants outperform C\(_3\) plants under high temperature and light intensity?  
(A) Rubisco has higher affinity for CO\(_2\)  
(B) Bundle sheath cells concentrate CO\(_2\) around Rubisco  
(C) Stomata remain open longer  
(D) CAM metabolism is used

\item [Human Physiology] Which part of the nephron is responsible for facultative water reabsorption under hormonal control?  
(A) Proximal tubule  
(B) Loop of Henle  
(C) Distal tubule  
(D) Collecting duct

\item [Ecology] In an ecosystem with 12% trophic efficiency, if producers capture 8000 J of energy, how much energy is available to secondary consumers?  
(A) 960 J  
(B) 96 J  
(C) 1152 J  
(D) 120 J

\item [Cell Biology] Which enzyme is responsible for proofreading and excision of mismatched nucleotides during DNA replication in prokaryotes?  
(A) DNA polymerase I  
(B) DNA polymerase III  
(C) Ligase  
(D) Helicase

\item [Genetics] Which principle explains inheritance of traits like human skin pigmentation, where multiple genes and environment interact?  
(A) Epistasis  
(B) Polygenic inheritance with environmental influence  
(C) Codominance  
(D) Incomplete dominance

\item [Plant Physiology] Which mineral deficiency leads to poor root growth due to its role in energy transfer reactions?  
(A) Nitrogen  
(B) Phosphorus  
(C) Magnesium  
(D) Potassium

\item [Human Physiology] Which hormone increases blood glucose by stimulating glycogenolysis in the liver?  
(A) Insulin  
(B) Glucagon  
(C) Cortisol  
(D) Thyroxine

\item [Diversity] Which group of animals shows radial symmetry in adults but bilateral symmetry in larvae?  
(A) Arthropoda  
(B) Echinodermata  
(C) Mollusca  
(D) Annelida

\item [Cell Biology] Which checkpoint ensures DNA damage is repaired before replication begins?  
(A) G1 checkpoint  
(B) G2/M checkpoint  
(C) Metaphase checkpoint  
(D) Cytokinesis checkpoint

\item [Ecology] Which principle explains why two species of barnacles can coexist on the same rock surface by occupying different tidal zones?  
(A) Competitive exclusion  
(B) Resource partitioning  
(C) Mutualism  
(D) Succession

\item [Human Physiology] Which hormone stimulates reabsorption of calcium in kidneys and increases intestinal absorption via vitamin D activation?  
(A) Calcitonin  
(B) Parathyroid hormone  
(C) Cortisol  
(D) Aldosterone

\item [Biotechnology] Which technique allows simultaneous measurement of expression levels of thousands of genes using hybridization?  
(A) PCR  
(B) DNA microarray  
(C) Gel electrophoresis  
(D) ELISA
\end{enumerate}
