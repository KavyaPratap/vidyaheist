\section*{Biology Set 3 - Paper 2 (Questions)}

\begin{enumerate}
\item [Diversity] Which feature explains why *Hemichordata* were once grouped with Chordata but later separated?  
(A) Presence of pharyngeal slits but absence of notochord  
(B) Presence of dorsal nerve cord throughout life  
(C) Segmentation of body muscles  
(D) Radial symmetry in adults

\item [Cell Biology] In eukaryotic cells, which checkpoint ensures spindle fibers are correctly attached before sister chromatids separate?  
(A) G1 checkpoint  
(B) G2/M checkpoint  
(C) Metaphase checkpoint  
(D) Cytokinesis checkpoint

\item [Genetics] A woman heterozygous for color blindness (X\(^N\)X\(^n\)) marries a man with normal vision (X\(^N\)Y). What is the probability their daughter will be color blind?  
(A) 0%  
(B) 25%  
(C) 50%  
(D) 100%

\item [Plant Physiology] Which mechanism explains why C\(_4\) plants have higher photosynthetic efficiency in tropical climates compared to C\(_3\) plants?  
(A) Rubisco has higher affinity for CO\(_2\)  
(B) Spatial separation of carbon fixation reduces photorespiration  
(C) Stomata remain open longer  
(D) CAM metabolism is used

\item [Human Physiology] Which part of the nephron is responsible for obligatory reabsorption of water and nutrients?  
(A) Proximal tubule  
(B) Loop of Henle  
(C) Distal tubule  
(D) Collecting duct

\item [Ecology] In an ecosystem with 10% trophic efficiency, if producers capture 5000 J of energy, how much energy is available to tertiary consumers?  
(A) 500 J  
(B) 50 J  
(C) 5 J  
(D) 0.5 J

\item [Cell Biology] Which enzyme is responsible for joining Okazaki fragments during DNA replication?  
(A) DNA polymerase I  
(B) DNA ligase  
(C) Primase  
(D) Helicase

\item [Genetics] Which principle explains inheritance of traits like human intelligence, influenced by multiple genes and environment?  
(A) Epistasis  
(B) Polygenic inheritance  
(C) Codominance  
(D) Gene?environment interaction

\item [Plant Physiology] Which mineral deficiency leads to stunted growth due to its role in ATP and nucleic acid synthesis?  
(A) Nitrogen  
(B) Phosphorus  
(C) Magnesium  
(D) Potassium

\item [Human Physiology] Which hormone increases blood calcium levels by stimulating osteoclast activity?  
(A) Calcitonin  
(B) Parathyroid hormone  
(C) Insulin  
(D) Thyroxine

\item [Diversity] Which group of animals shows metameric segmentation and closed circulatory system?  
(A) Arthropoda  
(B) Annelida  
(C) Mollusca  
(D) Platyhelminthes

\item [Cell Biology] Which organelle is involved in photorespiration along with chloroplast and mitochondria?  
(A) Peroxisome  
(B) Lysosome  
(C) Golgi apparatus  
(D) Vacuole

\item [Ecology] Which principle explains why two species with identical niches cannot coexist indefinitely?  
(A) Competitive exclusion principle  
(B) Resource partitioning  
(C) Mutualism  
(D) Succession

\item [Human Physiology] Which hormone stimulates reabsorption of sodium ions in kidney tubules?  
(A) Insulin  
(B) Aldosterone  
(C) Cortisol  
(D) ADH

\item [Biotechnology] Which technique uses fluorescently labeled dideoxynucleotides to terminate DNA synthesis for sequencing?  
(A) PCR  
(B) Sanger sequencing  
(C) Gel electrophoresis  
(D) ELISA
\end{enumerate}
