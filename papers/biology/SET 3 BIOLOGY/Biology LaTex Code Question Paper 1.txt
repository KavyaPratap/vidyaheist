\section*{Biology Set 3 - Paper 1 (Questions)}

\begin{enumerate}
\item [Diversity] Which feature explains why *Cephalochordata* are considered closer to vertebrates than *Urochordata*?  
(A) Presence of notochord throughout life  
(B) Presence of pharyngeal slits only in larval stage  
(C) Segmentation of body muscles  
(D) Radial symmetry in adults

\item [Cell Biology] In oxidative phosphorylation, which complex of the electron transport chain directly reduces oxygen to water?  
(A) Complex I  
(B) Complex II  
(C) Complex III  
(D) Complex IV

\item [Genetics] A woman heterozygous for hemophilia (X\(^H\)X\(^h\)) marries a normal man (X\(^H\)Y). What is the probability their son will have hemophilia?  
(A) 0%  
(B) 25%  
(C) 50%  
(D) 100%

\item [Plant Physiology] Which mechanism explains why CAM plants are more water?efficient than C\(_3\) plants?  
(A) Stomata open during day  
(B) Stomata open at night  
(C) Use of Rubisco at night  
(D) Direct fixation of CO\(_2\) into pyruvate

\item [Human Physiology] Which part of the nephron is crucial for generating hyperosmotic medullary interstitium?  
(A) Proximal tubule  
(B) Loop of Henle  
(C) Distal tubule  
(D) Collecting duct

\item [Ecology] In an ecosystem, if trophic efficiency is 15%, how much energy from 2000 J at producer level reaches secondary consumers?  
(A) 300 J  
(B) 225 J  
(C) 30 J  
(D) 450 J

\item [Cell Biology] Which enzyme is responsible for removing RNA primers and replacing them with DNA in prokaryotes?  
(A) DNA polymerase I  
(B) DNA polymerase III  
(C) Ligase  
(D) Primase

\item [Genetics] Which phenomenon explains inheritance of traits like human height, where multiple genes contribute additively?  
(A) Epistasis  
(B) Polygenic inheritance  
(C) Codominance  
(D) Incomplete dominance

\item [Plant Physiology] Which mineral deficiency leads to interveinal chlorosis in older leaves due to mobility?  
(A) Nitrogen  
(B) Magnesium  
(C) Iron  
(D) Calcium

\item [Human Physiology] Which hormone increases reabsorption of water by inserting aquaporins in collecting duct membranes?  
(A) Aldosterone  
(B) ADH  
(C) Cortisol  
(D) Insulin

\item [Diversity] Which group of fungi produces dikaryotic mycelium before karyogamy?  
(A) Zygomycota  
(B) Basidiomycota  
(C) Ascomycota  
(D) Chytridiomycota

\item [Cell Biology] Which checkpoint prevents anaphase until all kinetochores are properly attached?  
(A) G1 checkpoint  
(B) G2/M checkpoint  
(C) Metaphase checkpoint  
(D) Cytokinesis checkpoint

\item [Ecology] Which principle explains coexistence of warblers in the same tree by feeding at different heights?  
(A) Competitive exclusion  
(B) Resource partitioning  
(C) Mutualism  
(D) Succession

\item [Human Physiology] Which hormone stimulates gluconeogenesis and suppresses immune response?  
(A) Insulin  
(B) Cortisol  
(C) Glucagon  
(D) Thyroxine

\item [Biotechnology] Which technique uses fluorescent probes to detect specific DNA sequences in intact chromosomes?  
(A) PCR  
(B) FISH (Fluorescence in situ hybridization)  
(C) Gel electrophoresis  
(D) ELISA
\end{enumerate}
