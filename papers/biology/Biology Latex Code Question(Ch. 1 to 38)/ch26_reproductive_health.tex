\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]

\item The oral contraceptive pill (OCP) prevents pregnancy primarily by:
\\(A) Forming a physical barrier to sperm
\\(B) Suppressing ovulation via synthetic estrogen and progesterone inhibiting GnRH, FSH, and LH
\\(C) Preventing implantation only
\\(D) Destroying sperm in the vagina

\item IUD (intrauterine device) with copper prevents pregnancy by:
\\(A) Blocking the cervix physically
\\(B) Cu$^{2+}$ ions are spermicidal and the device prevents implantation
\\(C) Suppressing hormone production
\\(D) Preventing fertilization in the Fallopian tube only

\item Amniocentesis is a prenatal diagnostic technique used to:
\\(A) Determine blood group of fetus
\\(B) Analyze fetal cells in amniotic fluid for chromosomal abnormalities and genetic disorders
\\(C) Measure fetal growth by imaging
\\(D) Detect maternal infections

\item Which of the following STIs (Sexually Transmitted Infections) is caused by a bacterium?
\\(A) HIV/AIDS
\\(B) Hepatitis B
\\(C) Syphilis (\textit{Treponema pallidum})
\\(D) Genital herpes (HSV-2)

\item Assertion: MTP (Medical Termination of Pregnancy) is legally permitted in India under specific conditions.\\
Reason: The MTP Act, 1971 allows termination of pregnancy up to 20 weeks under medical supervision to prevent misuse.
\\(A) Both true, Reason is correct explanation
\\(B) Both true, Reason is not correct explanation
\\(C) Assertion true, Reason false
\\(D) Assertion false, Reason true

\item GIFT (Gamete Intrafallopian Transfer) differs from IVF in that:
\\(A) In GIFT, fertilization occurs in vitro in a lab
\\(B) In GIFT, eggs and sperm are transferred directly to the Fallopian tube where natural fertilization occurs
\\(C) GIFT uses donor eggs always
\\(D) GIFT involves hormonal suppression only

\item Vasectomy in males involves:
\\(A) Removal of the testis
\\(B) Surgical cutting and ligation of the vas deferens, preventing sperm from reaching semen
\\(C) Hormonal suppression of spermatogenesis
\\(D) Blockage of the epididymis

\item Which of the following is a barrier contraceptive method?
\\(A) Oral contraceptive pill
\\(B) IUD
\\(C) Male condom
\\(D) Hormonal implant

\item The Reproductive and Child Health Care (RCH) programme in India aims at:
\\(A) Only controlling population growth
\\(B) Creating awareness about reproductive health, reducing maternal and infant mortality, and family planning
\\(C) Providing IVF services to all couples
\\(D) Promoting adoption as contraception

\item ZIFT (Zygote Intrafallopian Transfer) involves:
\\(A) Transfer of embryo (8-cell stage) to uterus
\\(B) Transfer of zygote/early embryo to the Fallopian tube
\\(C) Transfer of gametes to the Fallopian tube
\\(D) Intracytoplasmic sperm injection

\item Which STI can be transmitted from mother to child during childbirth or breastfeeding?
\\(A) Gonorrhea only
\\(B) HIV, hepatitis B, and syphilis
\\(C) Chlamydia only
\\(D) HPV only

\item Emergency contraception (``morning after pill'') works by:
\\(A) Preventing fertilization by killing sperm
\\(B) High-dose progestin/estrogen delaying ovulation and/or preventing implantation
\\(C) Inducing early abortion of established pregnancy
\\(D) Creating a barrier in the cervix

\item The reason sex-selective abortion (female foeticide) is illegal in India is because:
\\(A) It is medically dangerous
\\(B) It disrupts the sex ratio and violates ethical and legal norms under the PCPNDT Act
\\(C) Amniocentesis is banned entirely
\\(D) It affects fertility of the mother

\item Match the following assisted reproductive technologies (ART) with their descriptions:\\
1. IVF $\rightarrow$ (a) Fertilization in a lab dish; embryo transferred to uterus\\
2. ICSI $\rightarrow$ (b) Single sperm injected directly into egg\\
3. GIFT $\rightarrow$ (c) Gametes transferred to Fallopian tube\\
4. Surrogacy $\rightarrow$ (d) Another woman carries the embryo to term
\\(A) 1-a, 2-b, 3-c, 4-d
\\(B) 1-b, 2-a, 3-d, 4-c
\\(C) 1-c, 2-d, 3-a, 4-b
\\(D) 1-d, 2-c, 3-b, 4-a

\item Infertility caused by blocked Fallopian tubes in females can be treated by:
\\(A) Hormone therapy alone
\\(B) IVF (In Vitro Fertilization) where fertilization bypasses the Fallopian tube
\\(C) Vasectomy reversal
\\(D) Intrauterine insemination (IUI)

\end{enumerate}
