\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]

\item Whittaker's five-kingdom classification is based on which primary criteria?
\\(A) Cell type, body organization, mode of nutrition, reproduction, phylogenetic relationships
\\(B) Cell type, habitat, mode of nutrition, locomotion
\\(C) Morphology, physiology, habitat, evolutionary history
\\(D) Cell wall composition, mode of reproduction, body size

\item Which kingdom includes organisms that are prokaryotic, lack membrane-bound nucleus, and include both autotrophs and heterotrophs?
\\(A) Protista
\\(B) Monera
\\(C) Fungi
\\(D) Plantae

\item \textit{Mycoplasma} is unique among bacteria because:
\\(A) It is photosynthetic
\\(B) It lacks a cell wall entirely
\\(C) It has a membrane-bound nucleus
\\(D) It reproduces only sexually

\item Which of the following is a correct match of organism and its kingdom?
\\(A) \textit{Euglena} -- Plantae
\\(B) \textit{Amoeba} -- Monera
\\(C) \textit{Paramecium} -- Protista
\\(D) \textit{Mucor} -- Monera

\item In fungi, the vegetative body is called:
\\(A) Thallus
\\(B) Mycelium
\\(C) Prothallus
\\(D) Rhizome

\item Fungi are placed in a separate kingdom because:
\\(A) They are unicellular like bacteria
\\(B) They have eukaryotic cells with heterotrophic nutrition and chitinous cell walls
\\(C) They perform photosynthesis using specialized pigments
\\(D) They have prokaryotic cells with peptidoglycan walls

\item Which of the following organisms causes red tides in the ocean?
\\(A) \textit{Trypanosoma}
\\(B) \textit{Gonyaulax}
\\(C) \textit{Plasmodium}
\\(D) \textit{Euglena}

\item Assertion: \textit{Anabaena} can fix atmospheric nitrogen.\\
Reason: \textit{Anabaena} is a cyanobacterium with specialized cells called heterocysts.
\\(A) Both true, Reason is correct explanation
\\(B) Both true, Reason is not correct explanation
\\(C) Assertion true, Reason false
\\(D) Assertion false, Reason true

\item Slime moulds were earlier classified with fungi but are now placed in Protista because:
\\(A) They lack a cell wall in vegetative phase
\\(B) They have chloroplasts
\\(C) They are unicellular
\\(D) They reproduce sexually

\item Which group of organisms forms the largest biomass on Earth?
\\(A) Fungi
\\(B) Bacteria
\\(C) Insects
\\(D) Plants

\item The cell wall of fungi is composed of:
\\(A) Cellulose
\\(B) Chitin
\\(C) Peptidoglycan
\\(D) Silica

\item Which of the following is an example of a archaebacterium that lives in extremely salty environments?
\\(A) \textit{Methanobacterium}
\\(B) \textit{Halobacterium}
\\(C) \textit{Thermoacidophile}
\\(D) \textit{Nostoc}

\item Match the following organisms with their correct kingdom:\\
1. \textit{Mucor} $\rightarrow$ (a) Fungi\\
2. \textit{Nostoc} $\rightarrow$ (b) Monera\\
3. \textit{Euglena} $\rightarrow$ (c) Protista\\
4. \textit{Agaricus} $\rightarrow$ (d) Fungi
\\(A) 1-a, 2-b, 3-c, 4-d
\\(B) 1-b, 2-a, 3-d, 4-c
\\(C) 1-c, 2-d, 3-a, 4-b
\\(D) 1-a, 2-c, 3-b, 4-d

\item Viroids differ from viruses in that:
\\(A) Viroids have a protein coat but no nucleic acid
\\(B) Viroids consist only of small circular RNA without a protein coat
\\(C) Viroids are larger than viruses
\\(D) Viroids infect animals only

\item Lichens are ecologically significant because they:
\\(A) Are primary consumers in food chains
\\(B) Act as pioneer species in primary succession on bare rocks
\\(C) Fix atmospheric nitrogen exclusively
\\(D) Decompose dead organic matter in soil

\end{enumerate}
