\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]

\item Steroid hormones exert their effects by:
\\(A) Binding to membrane receptors and activating second messengers
\\(B) Entering the cell and binding to intracellular receptors; the complex acts as a transcription factor
\\(C) Directly activating enzyme cascades in the cytoplasm
\\(D) Binding to ion channels on the cell membrane

\item The second messenger cAMP is produced when peptide hormones bind to their receptors because:
\\(A) The hormone enters the cell and stimulates adenylyl cyclase directly
\\(B) Receptor activation stimulates G-protein which activates adenylyl cyclase to convert ATP to cAMP
\\(C) cAMP is released from hormone-receptor complex
\\(D) The hormone directly phosphorylates proteins

\item Negative feedback regulation of the HPT (hypothalamus-pituitary-thyroid) axis means:
\\(A) High thyroxine stimulates more TSH and TRH production
\\(B) High thyroxine inhibits TSH from pituitary and TRH from hypothalamus
\\(C) Low TRH increases thyroxine production directly
\\(D) Pituitary TSH stimulates hypothalamic TRH production

\item Which hormone is produced by the pineal gland and regulates circadian rhythm?
\\(A) Serotonin
\\(B) Melatonin
\\(C) Dopamine
\\(D) ADH

\item Assertion: Diabetes insipidus is caused by deficiency of ADH, not insulin.\\
Reason: Without ADH, the collecting duct cannot reabsorb water, leading to production of large volumes of dilute urine.
\\(A) Both true, Reason is correct explanation
\\(B) Both true, Reason is not correct explanation
\\(C) Assertion true, Reason false
\\(D) Assertion false, Reason true

\item The adrenal cortex produces which of the following groups of hormones?
\\(A) Adrenaline and noradrenaline
\\(B) Glucocorticoids (cortisol), mineralocorticoids (aldosterone), and small amounts of sex steroids
\\(C) Insulin and glucagon
\\(D) FSH and LH

\item Acromegaly in adults results from:
\\(A) Deficiency of growth hormone (GH) in childhood
\\(B) Excess GH secretion after closure of epiphyseal plates (in adulthood)
\\(C) Deficiency of thyroxine
\\(D) Excess prolactin secretion

\item Insulin and glucagon are secreted by which cells of the islets of Langerhans respectively?
\\(A) Alpha cells (insulin) and beta cells (glucagon)
\\(B) Beta cells (insulin) and alpha cells (glucagon)
\\(C) Delta cells (insulin) and beta cells (glucagon)
\\(D) Beta cells (insulin) and delta cells (glucagon)

\item Cushing's syndrome results from chronic excess of:
\\(A) Aldosterone
\\(B) Cortisol (glucocorticoids)
\\(C) Thyroxine
\\(D) Growth hormone

\item Which hormone directly stimulates osteoclast activity and raises blood calcium?
\\(A) Calcitonin
\\(B) Parathyroid hormone (PTH)
\\(C) Thyroxine
\\(D) Aldosterone

\item Atrial Natriuretic Peptide (ANP) opposes the RAAS system by:
\\(A) Stimulating renin release
\\(B) Promoting Na$^+$ and water excretion, causing vasodilation and decreasing blood pressure
\\(C) Stimulating aldosterone secretion
\\(D) Increasing ADH secretion

\item Gigantism (in children) and acromegaly (in adults) are both caused by:
\\(A) Excess thyroid hormone
\\(B) Excess growth hormone (GH) secretion from pituitary
\\(C) Deficiency of somatostatin
\\(D) Excess IGF-1 from liver only

\item The zona glomerulosa of the adrenal cortex produces:
\\(A) Cortisol
\\(B) Androgens
\\(C) Aldosterone
\\(D) Adrenaline

\item Match the following hormones with their source glands:\\
1. Oxytocin $\rightarrow$ (a) Posterior pituitary (synthesized in hypothalamus)\\
2. FSH $\rightarrow$ (b) Anterior pituitary\\
3. Thyroxine $\rightarrow$ (c) Thyroid gland\\
4. Cortisol $\rightarrow$ (d) Adrenal cortex
\\(A) 1-a, 2-b, 3-c, 4-d
\\(B) 1-b, 2-a, 3-d, 4-c
\\(C) 1-c, 2-d, 3-a, 4-b
\\(D) 1-d, 2-c, 3-b, 4-a

\item A patient with Addison's disease (primary adrenal insufficiency) shows low cortisol and high ACTH because:
\\(A) Pituitary overproduces ACTH due to lack of negative feedback from cortisol
\\(B) Hypothalamus stops producing CRH
\\(C) Thyroid gland is overactive
\\(D) Adrenal medulla produces excess adrenaline

\end{enumerate}
