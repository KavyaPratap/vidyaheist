\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]

\item Which of the following is present in prokaryotic cells but absent in eukaryotic cells?
\\(A) Ribosome
\\(B) Cell membrane
\\(C) Mesosome
\\(D) DNA

\item The fluid mosaic model of cell membrane was proposed by:
\\(A) Watson and Crick
\\(B) Singer and Nicolson
\\(C) Schleiden and Schwann
\\(D) Robert Hooke

\item Which organelle is known as the ``powerhouse'' of the cell and has its own DNA and ribosomes?
\\(A) Nucleus
\\(B) Chloroplast
\\(C) Mitochondria
\\(D) Golgi apparatus

\item The unit membrane concept states that all biological membranes have a thickness of approximately:
\\(A) 5--7 nm
\\(B) 7.5--10 nm
\\(C) 20--25 nm
\\(D) 1--2 nm

\item Assertion: The nucleus is called the control center of the cell.\\
Reason: The nucleus contains DNA which directs all cellular activities through gene expression.
\\(A) Both true, Reason is correct explanation
\\(B) Both true, Reason is not correct explanation
\\(C) Assertion true, Reason false
\\(D) Assertion false, Reason true

\item The 9+2 arrangement of microtubules is found in:
\\(A) Centrioles
\\(B) Cilia and flagella (axoneme)
\\(C) Spindle fibres
\\(D) Intermediate filaments

\item Lysosomes are produced by which organelle?
\\(A) Endoplasmic reticulum alone
\\(B) Golgi apparatus (in coordination with ER)
\\(C) Mitochondria
\\(D) Nucleus

\item Which of the following is correct about the difference between 70S and 80S ribosomes?
\\(A) 70S in eukaryotes; 80S in prokaryotes
\\(B) 70S in prokaryotes and mitochondria; 80S in eukaryotic cytoplasm
\\(C) Both types are found in all cells
\\(D) 70S only in plant cells; 80S only in animal cells

\item Plasmodesmata are:
\\(A) Pores in the nuclear envelope
\\(B) Cytoplasmic connections between adjacent plant cells through cell walls
\\(C) Gap junctions in animal cells
\\(D) Channels in chloroplast membranes

\item The nucleolus is the site of:
\\(A) DNA replication
\\(B) Ribosomal RNA (rRNA) synthesis and ribosome assembly
\\(C) mRNA processing
\\(D) Protein synthesis

\item The primary cell wall of plants is composed mainly of:
\\(A) Chitin
\\(B) Cellulose, hemicellulose, and pectin
\\(C) Lignin and suberin
\\(D) Protein and lipid

\item Which membrane-bound organelle is involved in detoxification of drugs and alcohol in liver cells?
\\(A) Rough ER
\\(B) Smooth ER
\\(C) Lysosome
\\(D) Peroxisome

\item Tonoplast is the membrane that surrounds:
\\(A) Nucleus
\\(B) Vacuole
\\(C) Chloroplast
\\(D) Mitochondria

\item Match the following organelles with their functions:\\
1. Peroxisome $\rightarrow$ (a) Breakdown of fatty acids and H$_2$O$_2$\\
2. Centrosome $\rightarrow$ (b) Formation of spindle during cell division\\
3. Glyoxysome $\rightarrow$ (c) Conversion of fats to carbohydrates in germinating seeds\\
4. Nucleolus $\rightarrow$ (d) rRNA synthesis
\\(A) 1-a, 2-b, 3-c, 4-d
\\(B) 1-b, 2-c, 3-a, 4-d
\\(C) 1-d, 2-b, 3-c, 4-a
\\(D) 1-c, 2-a, 3-d, 4-b

\item The inner membrane of mitochondria is highly folded into cristae to:
\\(A) Increase the volume of the matrix
\\(B) Increase surface area for ATP synthase complexes and electron transport chain
\\(C) Provide structural rigidity
\\(D) Store calcium ions

\end{enumerate}
