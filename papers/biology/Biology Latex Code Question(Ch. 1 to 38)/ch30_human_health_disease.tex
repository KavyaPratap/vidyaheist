\begin{enumerate}[label=Q\arabic*.]

\item The life cycle of \textit{Plasmodium} in humans involves which primary site for asexual reproduction (schizogony)?
\\(A) Liver cells (hepatocytes) and RBCs
\\(B) WBCs only
\\(C) Spleen cells
\\(D) Kidney cells

\item Which of the following correctly describes the mechanism of action of antibiotics like penicillin?
\\(A) They disrupt the bacterial cell membrane
\\(B) They inhibit bacterial cell wall synthesis (peptidoglycan cross-linking)
\\(C) They block bacterial ribosome (50S subunit) protein synthesis
\\(D) They inhibit bacterial DNA replication

\item The primary immune response differs from the secondary immune response in that:
\\(A) The primary response is faster and produces more antibodies
\\(B) The secondary response is faster and stronger due to memory B and T cells
\\(C) The primary response involves T cells; secondary involves B cells
\\(D) There is no difference in kinetics

\item AIDS is caused by HIV, which primarily destroys:
\\(A) B lymphocytes
\\(B) CD4$^+$ T helper cells, crippling the immune system
\\(C) Natural killer cells
\\(D) Neutrophils

\item Assertion: Vaccines provide active immunity by stimulating the immune system to produce memory cells.\\
Reason: Vaccines contain antigens (attenuated or killed pathogens, or their components) that trigger an immune response without causing disease.
\\(A) Both true, Reason is correct explanation
\\(B) Both true, Reason is not correct explanation
\\(C) Assertion true, Reason false
\\(D) Assertion false, Reason true

\item Cancer cells differ from normal cells in that they:
\\(A) Undergo contact inhibition when touching neighboring cells
\\(B) Exhibit uncontrolled division, loss of contact inhibition, invasiveness, and metastasis
\\(C) Have lower metabolic rates than normal cells
\\(D) Undergo programmed cell death (apoptosis) more readily

\item The drug heroin (diacetylmorphine) causes addiction by:
\\(A) Stimulating dopamine release in the reward pathway
\\(B) Binding to opioid receptors, mimicking endorphins and causing euphoria; withdrawal causes severe symptoms
\\(C) Blocking serotonin reuptake
\\(D) Inhibiting acetylcholinesterase

\item Widal test is used to diagnose:
\\(A) Malaria
\\(B) Typhoid fever (\textit{Salmonella typhi} infection)
\\(C) Dengue fever
\\(D) Hepatitis B

\item Proto-oncogenes become oncogenes through:
\\(A) Deletion of tumor suppressor genes only
\\(B) Mutation, amplification, or translocation causing constitutive activation, driving uncontrolled growth
\\(C) DNA methylation silencing
\\(D) Viral integration near the gene always

\item The complement system in innate immunity functions by:
\\(A) Producing antibodies against pathogens
\\(B) Lysing pathogens via membrane attack complex (MAC), opsonization, and inflammation promotion
\\(C) Presenting antigens to T cells
\\(D) Producing interferons

\item Passive immunity is acquired by:
\\(A) Vaccination with attenuated pathogen
\\(B) Receiving pre-formed antibodies (e.g., antivenom, maternal antibodies via placenta/breast milk)
\\(C) Recovery from natural infection
\\(D) Booster vaccination

\item Interferons are produced by virus-infected cells and function to:
\\(A) Directly kill viruses
\\(B) Signal neighboring cells to activate antiviral defenses, inhibiting viral replication
\\(C) Stimulate B cell differentiation into plasma cells
\\(D) Activate the complement system

\item Which of the following is a correct match of disease and causative agent?
\\(A) Typhoid -- \textit{Plasmodium vivax}
\\(B) Ringworm -- \textit{Microsporum} (fungus)
\\(C) Pneumonia -- \textit{Wuchereria bancrofti}
\\(D) Malaria -- \textit{Salmonella typhi}

\item Match the following immune cells with their functions:\\
1. Cytotoxic T cells (CD8$^+$) $\rightarrow$ (a) Kill virus-infected cells and tumor cells\\
2. Helper T cells (CD4$^+$) $\rightarrow$ (b) Coordinate immune response; activate B and cytotoxic T cells\\
3. B cells $\rightarrow$ (c) Differentiate into plasma cells producing antibodies\\
4. Natural killer (NK) cells $\rightarrow$ (d) Innate immune cells killing infected/tumor cells without MHC restriction
\\(A) 1-a, 2-b, 3-c, 4-d
\\(B) 1-b, 2-a, 3-d, 4-c
\\(C) 1-c, 2-d, 3-a, 4-b
\\(D) 1-d, 2-c, 3-b, 4-a

\item Metastasis in cancer refers to:
\\(A) Uncontrolled division of cells at the primary tumor site
\\(B) Spread of cancer cells from the primary tumor to distant organs via blood or lymph
\\(C) Angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation) in the tumor
\\(D) Loss of contact inhibition at the primary site

\end{enumerate}
